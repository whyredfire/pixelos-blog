 Let's dive into the changelog:

### System Updates:

- Merged June Android Open Source Project (AOSP) patches to ensure your device stays up-to-date with the latest security fixes and optimizations.

### GMS and Face Unlock:

- Switched to AOSPA's GMS (Google Mobile Services) to improve overall compatibility with Google apps and services, offering a seamless user experience.

- Adopted AOSPA's face unlock implementation, providing faster and more reliable face recognition for unlocking your device securely.

### Launcher Enhancements:

- Fixed an issue where the launcher in light mode would still display dark search results, ensuring a consistent and visually pleasing experience.

- Addressed a bug where the wallpapers section would not open from the home screen, allowing you to customize your device's look effortlessly.

### Monet Boot Animation:

- Brought back the beloved Monet boot animation to add a touch of elegance and uniqueness to your PixelOS device during startup.

### Lockscreen Improvements:

- Users can now pick lockscreen shortcuts, granting quick access to their favorite apps directly from the lock screen for added convenience.

- (Possibly) Fixed an issue where two status bars would display, eliminating any potential visual clutter.

### Status Bar Updates:

- Enabled the new plugin status bar chip, enhancing the status bar's functionality and providing more informative notifications.

- Introduced the explicit media indicator to easily identify which app is currently playing media from the status bar.

### Display and Dark Mode Enhancements:

- Made improvements to Pitch Black mode, refining the dark theme experience for better readability and aesthetics.

- Introducing the Emoji wallpaper, adding a touch of fun and personality to your device's display.

- Enabled the new wallpaper picker UI for lockscreen and home screen, simplifying the process of choosing your favorite backgrounds.

- Users can now customize the lock screen clock, allowing you to personalize your device's appearance further.

We hope you enjoy these updates and enhancements as much as we enjoyed working on them. Your support and feedback are essential in making PixelOS better with each release.
